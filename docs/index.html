Overview

Why DASH?

Natural logs contain realistic context but few long-tail conflicts. DASH focuses adversarial exposure near the current policy’s weak regions and avoids treating every generated collision as equally useful.

01

Policy-aligned stress

Candidate futures are ranked by closed-loop interaction risk rather than selected only by likelihood or static geometry.

02

Traceable local edits

Residual refinement stays within a bounded neighborhood of a selected motion-prior trajectory, preserving an interpretable origin.

03

Risk-conditioned control

A base actor handles ordinary driving while an online gate activates residual correction only when local risk rises.

Method

Rank first, refine locally, train in closed loop.

The public page should show one clear method figure, not every architecture and diagnostic panel from the paper.

Generation

Multimodal candidate bank

Generate 32 motion-prior futures for one interacting agent while holding history, map context, and other agents fixed.

Selection

Learned risk ranking

Order candidates by policy-relevant interaction risk and retain the Top-4 modes for local refinement.

Refinement

Bounded residual editing

Strengthen the selected interaction with smoothed, clipped displacement updates under a 3.0 m per-step bound.

Training

Risk-gated residual TD3

Combine a nominal base action with state-dependent residual correction and risk-structured replay.
